History of Gender-Based Violence (GBV)

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) has existed for thousands of years and is rooted in unequal power relations between genders. Historically, many societies placed women and girls in subordinate positions, limiting their access to education, property, and decision-making. Throughout the 19th and 20th centuries, awareness grew, especially as women fought for voting rights, property rights, and equal representation. The modern fight against GBV gained momentum in the 1990s when international bodies like the United Nations officially recognized violence against women as a violation of human rights.
